## STEP 3 — Review Acquisition Strategy

To dominate locally, reviews are the single biggest ranking factor alongside proximity and relevance. You need consistent flow.

### Who to ask first (warm leads — highest conversion):
1. Everyone you have already done work for — EJ Cuts, Maison De May, anyone else
2. Friends and family who have interacted with your business
3. Anyone you have had a call or meeting with, even if they didn't buy
4. Anyone you know in Peterborough or Cambridgeshire who can vouch for you

### How to ask (exact script):
> "Hey [name], hope you're well. I've just set up my Google Business Profile for Axio Marketing. It would mean a lot if you could leave us a quick review — it genuinely makes a huge difference when we're just starting out. Here's the direct link: [your GBP review link]. Even just a sentence or two is perfect. Thanks so much."

### Getting your review link:
1. Open your GBP dashboard
2. Click **Ask for reviews**
3. Copy the short link (looks like: g.page/r/xxx/review)
4. Save this link — you'll use it constantly

### Review velocity matters:
- Google rewards consistent review flow, not one burst then nothing
- Target: **5 reviews in week 1** (warm contacts), then **2–3 per month minimum**
- At 3/month, you'll have 36 reviews in a year — enough to rank competitively in Peterborough

### Review card strategy:
- Create a physical card (use your print services) with:
  - "How did we do?" headline
  - QR code linking to your review page
  - Your logo
- Hand these to every client when you deliver a project

### Responding to reviews:
- Respond to every review within 24 hours — positive AND negative
- Positive response template:
  > "Thank you so much, [name]! It was a pleasure working with you and we're thrilled you love the result. Looking forward to supporting [business name] as it grows."
- Never copy-paste the same response — Google detects it and it looks bad to prospects
